7.At very low pressure and high temperature, the real gas behaves like ideal gas. Why?
Ans.
An ideal gas is one which has Zero volume of molecule and no intermolecular forces.
Now:
1) At very low pressure, the volume of gas is large so that the volume of molecule is
negligible compared to volume of gas.
2) At very high temperature, the kinetic energy of molecules is very large and effect of
intermolecular forces can be neglected.
Hence real gases behave as an ideal gas at low pressure and high temperature.
