5. A gas in equilibrium has uniform density and pressure throughout its volume. This
is strictly true only if there are no external influences. A gas column under gravity, for
example, does not have uniform density (and pressure). As you might expect, its
density decreases with height. The precise dependence is given by the so-called law of
atmospheres
